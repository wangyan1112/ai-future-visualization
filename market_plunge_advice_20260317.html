Market Plunge: A Calm Perspective

"In the short run, the market is a voting machine but in the long run, it is a weighing machine." — Benjamin Graham

Today's market movement has undoubtedly caused anxiety for many. Seeing red across the board is never pleasant, but panic is the enemy of profit. As long-term investors, we must separate price from value. The fundamental value of great companies does not change by 5% in a single day.

"Be fearful when others are greedy, and greedy when others are fearful."

Strategy for Full Position Holders

Do Not Panic Sell. If you are fully invested, today is a test of conviction. Review your holdings: has the business logic changed? If not, ignore the noise. Selling into a panic often locks in losses at the worst possible time. Turn off your trading app, go for a walk, and focus on the long-term horizon (3-5 years). The market always recovers.

Strategy for Half Position Holders

Opportunity Knocks. This is the moment you've been waiting for. With cash on hand, you are in a position of strength. Do not rush to buy everything at once—we don't know where the bottom is. Instead, start deploying capital slowly into high-quality companies that are now on sale. Use a grid trading strategy or simply buy in tranches (e.g., 10% of remaining cash every 5% drop).

Remember, volatility is the price we pay for superior long-term returns. Stay disciplined, stay rational.
